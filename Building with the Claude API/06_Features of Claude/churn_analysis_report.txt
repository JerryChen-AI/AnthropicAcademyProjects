                    STREAMING SERVICE CHURN ANALYSIS
                          DETAILED FINDINGS REPORT

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This analysis examined 500 streaming service customers to identify the major 
drivers of customer churn. The overall churn rate is 38.6%, with 193 customers 
having churned and 307 remaining active.

Through statistical analysis (t-tests and chi-square tests), we identified 
seven significant factors that distinguish churned customers from active ones.


MAJOR CHURN DRIVERS (Ranked by Impact)
================================================================================

1. CUSTOMER SERVICE INTERACTIONS - MOST CRITICAL DRIVER
   Impact: +27.7% (p < 0.001) *** HIGHLY SIGNIFICANT ***
   ------------------------------------------------------------------------
   - Churned customers: 3.18 interactions/year (average)
   - Active customers: 2.49 interactions/year (average)
   - Interpretation: Churned users contact customer service 28% MORE often
   
   KEY INSIGHT: High customer service interactions indicate unresolved issues,
   technical problems, or dissatisfaction. This is the #1 predictor of churn.
   
   RECOMMENDATION: 
   - Implement proactive outreach for customers with 3+ interactions
   - Improve first-contact resolution rates
   - Analyze common complaint patterns to address root causes


2. VIEWING HOURS - ENGAGEMENT INDICATOR
   Impact: -20.0% (p < 0.001) *** HIGHLY SIGNIFICANT ***
   ------------------------------------------------------------------------
   - Churned customers: 66.6 hours/month (average)
   - Active customers: 83.2 hours/month (average)
   - Interpretation: Churned users watch 20% LESS content
   
   KEY INSIGHT: Lower viewing hours signal disengagement before churn occurs.
   This is a strong early warning indicator.
   
   RECOMMENDATION:
   - Set up automated alerts for users whose viewing drops below 70 hours/month
   - Send personalized content recommendations to re-engage
   - Offer limited-time promotions when viewing drops


3. BINGE WATCHING SESSIONS - ENGAGEMENT DEPTH
   Impact: -19.7% (p < 0.001) *** HIGHLY SIGNIFICANT ***
   ------------------------------------------------------------------------
   - Churned customers: 6.2 sessions/month (average)
   - Active customers: 7.7 sessions/month (average)
   - Interpretation: Churned users have 20% FEWER binge sessions
   
   KEY INSIGHT: Binge watching indicates high engagement and content satisfaction.
   Loss of binge behavior suggests declining interest.
   
   RECOMMENDATION:
   - Promote binge-worthy series to at-risk users
   - Create "Continue Watching" nudges to encourage completion
   - Highlight new seasons of previously binged content


4. SUBSCRIPTION TIER - PRICING & VALUE PERCEPTION
   Impact: 19.4% difference (p = 0.009) ** SIGNIFICANT **
   ------------------------------------------------------------------------
   Churn rates by tier:
   - Basic tier: 43.5% churn (HIGHEST RISK)
   - Standard tier: 39.5% churn
   - Premium tier: 24.1% churn (LOWEST RISK)
   
   KEY INSIGHT: Premium subscribers are nearly 2x less likely to churn than
   Basic subscribers. Higher investment creates stronger commitment.
   
   RECOMMENDATION:
   - Offer upgrade incentives to Basic tier users
   - Create a "trial Premium features" campaign
   - Emphasize Premium value proposition (4K, multiple screens, etc.)
   - Consider Basic tier improvements to reduce churn at entry level


5. UNIQUE TITLES WATCHED - CONTENT DIVERSITY
   Impact: -18.1% (p < 0.001) *** HIGHLY SIGNIFICANT ***
   ------------------------------------------------------------------------
   - Churned customers: 19.5 titles/month (average)
   - Active customers: 23.7 titles/month (average)
   - Interpretation: Churned users explore 18% FEWER unique titles
   
   KEY INSIGHT: Limited content exploration suggests users aren't finding
   enough interesting content, potentially indicating catalog dissatisfaction.
   
   RECOMMENDATION:
   - Improve content discovery and recommendation algorithms
   - Create curated collections for different user profiles
   - Highlight hidden gems and diverse content options
   - Expand catalog in underperforming genres


6. AVERAGE SESSION DURATION - ENGAGEMENT QUALITY
   Impact: -14.4% (p < 0.001) *** HIGHLY SIGNIFICANT ***
   ------------------------------------------------------------------------
   - Churned customers: 49.4 minutes/session (average)
   - Active customers: 57.8 minutes/session (average)
   - Interpretation: Churned users have 14% SHORTER sessions
   
   KEY INSIGHT: Shorter sessions may indicate interrupted viewing, lack of
   compelling content, or technical issues causing early abandonment.
   
   RECOMMENDATION:
   - Investigate technical issues causing early session termination
   - Analyze content that drives longer session durations
   - Implement "Up Next" suggestions to extend sessions


7. MONTHLY COST - PRICE SENSITIVITY
   Impact: -7.7% (p = 0.005) ** SIGNIFICANT **
   ------------------------------------------------------------------------
   - Churned customers: $11.18/month (average)
   - Active customers: $12.11/month (average)
   - Interpretation: Churned users pay slightly less on average
   
   KEY INSIGHT: This is primarily driven by subscription tier differences.
   Lower-tier customers perceive less value and churn more.


ADDITIONAL FINDINGS
================================================================================

TOP GENRE PREFERENCES (Not Statistically Significant, p = 0.118)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
While not reaching statistical significance, notable patterns emerged:
- Horror viewers: 52.3% churn (highest)
- Thriller viewers: 48.3% churn
- Documentary viewers: 25.9% churn (lowest)
- Comedy viewers: 33.0% churn

INSIGHT: Genre preference alone doesn't predict churn, but combined with low
engagement metrics, it may help target retention efforts.


CUSTOMER SEGMENTS AT HIGHEST RISK
================================================================================

Profile of High-Risk Churner:
- Subscription: Basic tier
- Viewing Hours: < 70 hours/month
- Binge Sessions: < 6 per month
- Customer Service: 3+ interactions/year
- Unique Titles: < 20 per month
- Session Duration: < 50 minutes average

ESTIMATED AT-RISK POPULATION: ~40-45% of Basic tier subscribers

================================================================================

1. Deploy customer service quality improvement initiative
2. Create automated alerts for declining engagement metrics
3. Launch Basic → Premium upgrade campaign with trial period

SHORT-TERM ACTIONS (1-3 months):

5. Design targeted re-engagement campaigns for low-viewing users
6. Improve content recommendation algorithms

LONG-TERM ACTIONS (3-6 months):

9. Expand content catalog in high-churn genres
10. Develop personalized retention offers based on viewing patterns


EXPECTED IMPACT
================================================================================

If these recommendations are implemented:

TECHNICAL NOTES
================================================================================
- Sample size: 500 customers
- Statistical methods: Independent t-tests, chi-square tests
- Significance level: α = 0.05
- All p-values < 0.05 indicate statistical significance
- Effect sizes calculated as percentage differences between groups


CONCLUSION
================================================================================

Customer service issues and declining engagement metrics are the primary drivers
of churn in this streaming service. The analysis reveals that churn is 
predictable and preventable through proactive monitoring and targeted 
interventions. 

Immediate focus should be on:
1. Resolving customer service pain points
2. Re-engaging users with declining viewing patterns
3. Upgrading Basic tier customers to higher value tiers

These data-driven insights provide a clear roadmap for reducing churn and
improving customer lifetime value.
